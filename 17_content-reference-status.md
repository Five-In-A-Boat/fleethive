## Purpose and status definitions

This file exists to stop the build, copy, and design work from smuggling fiction into launch.

The governing question for every visible asset is not “does this look good?” It is “what is this allowed to mean in public?”

### Document status

- **Draft / working document:** usable now as a decision filter, but still waiting on asset-level approvals.

### Controlled asset-status vocabulary

| Status label | Meaning | Launch use |
|---|---|---|
| **Approved for launch** | Safe to use publicly in its current class, provided the final asset is real, current, and on-brand | Yes |
| **Approved with qualification** | Usable only if the copy, caption, or surrounding context makes the limitation explicit | Yes, with guardrails |
| **Pending asset approval** | Strategically valid asset type, but no approved source file or asset pack is available yet | Not until specific asset is approved |
| **Placeholder / concept only** | Can be used internally to guide design direction, but must not appear in public launch output as proof | No |
| **Blocked for launch** | Must not appear publicly because it overstates product reality, proof strength, or market maturity | No |
| **Unknown / needs validation** | Asset type or interpretation is unresolved; default to non-use until clarified | No by default |

### Operating rules

- **Recommendation:** Real current product evidence outranks decorative polish.
- **Recommendation:** Product-native proof outranks partner-scale proof.
- **Recommendation:** Trade-route assets outrank household-route assets until the household route is explicitly confirmed for launch.
- **Recommendation:** Any asset that changes the perceived category of FleetHive must be treated with extra caution.

## Allowed status labels and meanings

### Approved for launch

Use publicly with normal QA. The asset still has to be real, current, readable, and contextually honest.

### Approved with qualification

Use publicly only when one or more of the following is true:

- the source owner is labelled
- the audience scope is limited
- the surrounding copy narrows the claim
- the asset is clearly contextual rather than headline proof

### Pending asset approval

The asset class is strategically valid, but the specific source file or approved final version is missing.

### Placeholder / concept only

Keep inside Figma, briefs, or internal review if needed. Do not publish. Use this status for internal compositional ideas that have no approved real-world source asset.

### Blocked for launch

Do not publish. Replacement is required.

### Unknown / needs validation

No public use until clarified. Unknown does not equal “probably okay.”

## Launch-safe assets

These are the safest classes of visible assets to build the launch around.

### Safe now

- **Real current product screenshots** showing reminders, records, cost visibility, service workflow, booking-related actions, and timeline/history views
- **Route cards** that help visitors self-select between trades and households
- **Workflow proof cards** tied to present-tense product behaviour
- **“What happens next” modules** that set onboarding expectations clearly
- **Typography, colour, spacing, icon, and theme tokens** from the design-language system
- **Trade-first page visuals** that keep the product category anchored in maintenance, running costs, reminders, records, and supplier-decision control
- **Legal/compliance references** that preserve the truth-pack constraints

### Safe only if real asset files are approved

- final logo files and lockups
- final screenshot pack
- final approved testimonial pack
- any documentary photography
- any partner logos used with precise relationship wording

## Assets that require qualification or replacement

These assets are not automatically banned, but they are not safe as naked proof.

### Require qualification

- customer testimonials
- partner logos
- partner-enabled booking / approval / payment visuals
- quantified savings claims
- discount ranges
- service-network scale figures
- historical-data scale figures
- documentary photography used near proof-heavy sections
- diagrams or abstract visuals that could be mistaken for feature proof
- comparison tables that imply competitive superiority without evidence

### Replace if the qualification cannot be made clear

- any stat without source ownership
- any quote without clear attribution and audience relevance
- any visual that makes FleetHive look like telematics or enterprise command software
- any offer or pricing block that depends on unresolved packaging language

## Assets not approved for launch use

These must not appear publicly in the launch build.

- telematics, tracking, live-map, route-line, radar, dispatch, or command-centre imagery
- fake or speculative product UI
- roadmap modules rendered as current product
- broad “all-in-one fleet management platform” framing as visual or verbal proof
- customer-logo strips or “trusted by” rows without explicit permission and relevance
- awards, badges, accreditations, and review widgets without approved source assets
- stock-office photography, handshake imagery, smiling-team filler, and glamour vehicle photography
- hero motion or decorative data theatre designed to make the company look bigger than the product truth supports
- pricing tables, “free” claims, or plan grids while commercial packaging remains unresolved
- blended household-and-business proof blocks that flatten audience distinctions

## Implications for copy, design, and proof blocks

### Copy implications

- **Recommendation:** Use the safest present-tense category frame: maintenance and running-cost control.
- **Recommendation:** Separate product-native behaviour from partner-enabled capability every time booking, approval, payment, or network scale appears.
- **Recommendation:** Keep trade-route proof trade-specific.
- **Recommendation:** Do not let household language or proof leak into the primary trade story unless the page is explicitly routed.
- **Recommendation:** Avoid “largest”, “industry-leading”, “all-in-one”, “complete visibility”, or similar category-overreach language.

### Design implications

- **Recommendation:** The homepage should route before it claims.
- **Recommendation:** The hero should use one real screenshot stage, not a speculative composite.
- **Recommendation:** Proof sections should use source-type labels so a reader can tell whether something is product-native, partner-enabled, customer-quoted, or future-state.
- **Recommendation:** Household visuals should remain visually subordinate until the route is confirmed for launch.
- **Recommendation:** Fewer truthful sections are better than a fuller page built from unresolved proof.

### Proof-block implications

- **Recommendation:** Lead with workflow proof before numerical proof.
- **Recommendation:** Use only one or two carefully chosen quantified items per page section, and only when source ownership is explicit.
- **Recommendation:** Treat testimonials as supporting trust, not as substitutes for product explanation.
- **Recommendation:** Do not build proof walls out of mixed source types with no visible qualification.

## Handoff payload for page briefs, copy, and build

### Page briefs

- **Home / router:** use route cards plus one real screenshot stage; no proof wall; no mixed-audience headline proof.
- **Trade page:** screenshot-first, workflow-first, one approved trade testimonial at most, explicit next-step module.
- **Household page:** do not brief as launch-essential unless route, screenshots, and CTA path are all confirmed.
- **Product / how-it-works:** use sequences of real UI moments and clearly labelled workflow steps.
- **Use-cases / who-it’s-for:** qualification assets only; no speculative maturity cues.
- **Onboarding / sign-up entry:** expectation-setting modules outrank decorative assets.

### Copy deck

- **Carry forward as safe:** present-tense workflow, trade fit, maintenance/admin/cost-control framing, explicit next-step language.
- **Carry forward as conditional:** testimonials, scale numbers, savings, discount ranges, partner mentions.
- **Carry forward as blocked:** broad fleet-platform claims, unresolved pricing language, roadmap features as current truth.

### Build brief

- **Build first:** nav, route cards, hero screenshot stage, workflow proof cards, source-labelled proof cards, expectation module, route-aware CTA areas.
- **Do not build yet:** logo-wall block, awards block, pricing table, telematics-style visual layer, speculative animation system, roadmap showcase block.
- **Implementation rule:** every proof-bearing component should have a field for source owner and status label, so unresolved proof cannot be published accidentally.

### Open validation requests

- final approved logo files and usage rules
- approved screenshot pack
- approved testimonial list by audience segment
- partner-logo usage permissions and wording rules
- final pricing / plan / trial / “member” language
- final CTA wording once onboarding motion is confirmed
- launch decision on whether the household route is live at day one
