## Executive summary

- **Fact:** The website’s core job is not broad awareness. Its job is to move qualified UK visitors into the FleetHive onboarding flow.
- **Fact:** FleetHive currently appears to serve both households and small businesses, while the current commercial priority is believed to be SME fleets.
- **Inference:** Website success will depend less on looking like a mature SaaS brand and more on three practical outcomes: clear fit, low perceived onboarding friction, and a credible path into the product.
- **Recommendation:** The site should be judged first by whether the right visitor understands who FleetHive is for, what it helps them do now, and what to do next.
- **Recommendation:** Raw signup volume alone should not be treated as success if the site achieves that volume by creating confusion, attracting the wrong audience, or overpromising product maturity.
- **Placeholder:** “500 signups” is a rough commercial aspiration supplied in operator steering, not yet a validated success threshold.

## Overall business objective

- **Fact:** The stated primary business objective for the site is conversion.
- **Fact:** The manifest positions the site as a lean marketing system whose purpose is to drive sign up / onboarding rather than diffuse awareness.
- **Inference:** The site is successful only if it increases the number of qualified visitors who take a meaningful step into the product journey.
- **Recommendation:** Define website success as: **turning qualified traffic into started onboarding for the right users, through clear positioning, credible product framing, and low-friction next steps.**
- **Recommendation:** Treat unqualified traffic, vanity engagement, and design-led admiration as secondary at best.

### Business-objective pass / fail test

- **Success:** More of the right visitors reach a committed onboarding step with a clear expectation of what FleetHive does and what happens next.
- **Failure:** Visitors leave impressed by the brand but unclear on fit, unclear on the offer, or uncertain whether the product is live and relevant to them.

## Primary conversion goal

- **Fact:** The primary conversion event is sign up / onboarding.
- **Unknown / needs validation:** The exact product event that should count as the canonical conversion is not yet fixed in a canonical truth file.
- **Recommendation:** Until that definition is confirmed, treat the primary website conversion as the **first committed onboarding action** available to the user, such as account creation, onboarding start, or equivalent entry into the live product flow.
- **Recommendation:** The primary CTA should be unmistakable, repeated consistently, and matched to the real onboarding mechanics rather than to an aspirational future flow.
- **Recommendation:** The site should reduce hesitation around starting by making the next step feel concrete, bounded, and worth doing now.

### What must be true for the primary conversion goal to be credible

- **Recommendation:** The visitor must understand who FleetHive is for.
- **Recommendation:** The visitor must understand the present-day product value, not just the vision.
- **Recommendation:** The visitor must understand enough about the onboarding step to begin without fearing hidden complexity.
- **Recommendation:** The CTA language and the destination page must align. A “Sign up” CTA must not land on an ambiguous sales detour unless that is genuinely the current route.

## Secondary conversion goals

- **Fact:** Operator steering names subscription as the secondary conversion event.
- **Fact:** The client discovery kickoff also frames retention as a secondary website outcome.
- **Conflict note:** Subscription and retention are related but not identical. Subscription is a commercial event; retention is a post-conversion product-health outcome.
- **Recommendation:** Treat secondary conversion as a ladder rather than a single number.

### Recommended secondary conversion ladder

1. **Recommendation:** **Subscription start or plan selection** where that action is genuinely available and measurable.
2. **Recommendation:** **Qualified assisted route** for users who are better served by demo, walkthrough, or sales-assisted onboarding.
3. **Recommendation:** **Early activation signal** such as adding a first vehicle or completing the first meaningful setup step.
4. **Recommendation:** **Early return / retention signal** to indicate the website is attracting users who can reach value, not just click through.

- **Unknown / needs validation:** Which of these steps are technically measurable today and which must wait for product instrumentation.

## Audience routing goals

- **Fact:** Current inputs indicate two live audience realities: consumer / household users and small-business users.
- **Fact:** Current commercial priority appears to favour SME fleets.
- **Inference:** The site likely cannot maximise clarity if it speaks to both groups in one undifferentiated voice.
- **Recommendation:** Audience routing should help visitors self-identify quickly without forcing the site to pretend the ICP decision is already complete.

### Audience-routing success criteria

- **Success:** A household user can quickly tell whether FleetHive helps them manage multiple vehicles without being pushed into enterprise or sales-heavy framing.
- **Success:** A small-business user can quickly reach business-relevant content without wading through household-first language.
- **Success:** Routing helps each segment reach the most credible CTA path for them.
- **Failure:** The homepage collapses two distinct motivations into vague “for everyone with vehicles” language.
- **Failure:** Segment pages, if introduced, become near-duplicates with swapped nouns instead of materially different qualification paths.

## Page-level success criteria

### 1) Home page

**Page job**
- **Fact:** The home page is the primary entry point into the site’s conversion system.
- **Recommendation:** Its job is to establish fit, practical value, and the next step fast.

**Success criteria**
- **Success:** Within seconds, the visitor understands that FleetHive helps UK users who manage more than one vehicle reduce admin, maintenance friction, and running-cost chaos.
- **Success:** The home page signals whether the visitor is closer to the household path or the small-business path.
- **Success:** The primary CTA is obvious and appears early.
- **Success:** The page creates confidence that FleetHive is a real current product, not just an idea or future platform story.
- **Success:** The page narrows attention toward the next step instead of trying to explain every feature, proof point, and future module.

**Failure signals**
- **Failure:** The page uses broad fleet-tech language without clarifying the actual user problem.
- **Failure:** The audience fit remains fuzzy after the hero section.
- **Failure:** The page presents multiple equally weighted next steps that compete with sign up / onboarding.
- **Failure:** The page implies a level of product maturity or breadth that later pages cannot support.

### 2) Product / how-it-works page

**Page job**
- **Recommendation:** Explain what FleetHive actually does today, how it works in practice, and why it is worth adopting instead of continuing with ad hoc manual management.

**Success criteria**
- **Success:** The page shows current live capability clearly enough that a user can picture using the product.
- **Success:** The page distinguishes present-day functionality from premium or future-facing modules.
- **Success:** The page reduces the objection that “it is quicker just to ring the garage” by showing practical simplification, not feature theatre.
- **Success:** The page makes clear what actions the user can take inside the product and what work is still required from them.
- **Success:** Partner-enabled actions and FleetHive-native product actions are not blurred together carelessly.

**Failure signals**
- **Failure:** The page becomes a generic feature grid with no real workflow logic.
- **Failure:** The page treats aspirational modules as though they are already live.
- **Failure:** The page hides setup effort or creates false expectations about automation.

### 3) Use-cases / who-it’s-for page

**Page job**
- **Recommendation:** Help visitors self-qualify by use case, vehicle context, and operational need.

**Success criteria**
- **Success:** The page helps visitors decide whether FleetHive is meant for their situation.
- **Success:** The page translates product value into situation-specific outcomes rather than repeating homepage copy.
- **Success:** The page makes the difference between household and business value tangible if both audiences remain in scope.
- **Success:** The page channels the right visitor toward the right next step.

**Failure signals**
- **Failure:** The page tries to be a persona museum for every conceivable user type.
- **Failure:** The page lists segments without changing the message, objections handled, or CTA logic.
- **Failure:** The page creates additional audience ambiguity instead of resolving it.

### 4) Sign-up / onboarding entry page

**Page job**
- **Fact:** This page is the most conversion-critical page in the current working IA.
- **Recommendation:** Its job is to turn intent into action by reducing ambiguity and perceived setup burden.

**Success criteria**
- **Success:** The page explains what happens next in plain language.
- **Success:** The page sets expectations about effort, data required, and any free-versus-paid logic that materially affects the decision to continue.
- **Success:** The page reassures the visitor that they are starting a real, current flow rather than entering a vague lead form.
- **Success:** The page removes avoidable friction and surprise.
- **Success:** Any alternative route, such as demo or support, is clearly secondary unless business reality proves otherwise.

**Failure signals**
- **Failure:** The page hides commitment level, pricing implications, or required inputs until too late.
- **Failure:** The page introduces trust gaps right at the moment of commitment.
- **Failure:** The page mixes onboarding, sales, and education so heavily that the next step becomes unclear.

### 5) Conditional segment pages

- **Placeholder:** Segment-specific pages are not yet fixed in the page architecture, but operator steering suggests they may be needed.
- **Recommendation:** If separate household and small-business pages are introduced, each page must carry a distinct qualification job.

**Household page success**
- **Success:** It makes multi-vehicle household coordination feel easier, calmer, and more manageable.
- **Success:** It avoids business jargon and does not overcomplicate the value proposition.

**Small-business page success**
- **Success:** It makes cost control, oversight, repeatability, and reduced admin feel credible for small fleets.
- **Success:** It signals operational practicality rather than enterprise-theatre positioning.

**Failure for either page**
- **Failure:** The page is just a reskinned duplicate of the other segment page.

## Five-second clarity test

Within roughly five seconds of arriving on the site, the right visitor should be able to understand all of the following:

- **Fact:** FleetHive is a UK-focused product for people or businesses managing more than one vehicle.
- **Fact:** It helps organise and control the practical costs and admin of keeping those vehicles running.
- **Fact:** It is a current product with a real next step available now.
- **Recommendation:** The visitor should also be able to tell whether they are on the right path directly or need the household / business route.

### What the site must deliberately not try to do in the first five seconds

- **Recommendation:** Do not try to explain the entire roadmap.
- **Recommendation:** Do not try to prove every claim at once.
- **Recommendation:** Do not try to sound like a broad enterprise fleet platform unless later truth and strategy work justify that position.
- **Recommendation:** Do not try to speak equally to every vehicle owner in the market.

## Explicit non-goals

- **Fact:** The manifest already states that “500 signups” should not be treated as the project goal by default.
- **Recommendation:** Do not optimise for traffic, impressions, or polished SaaS aesthetics in isolation from qualified conversion.
- **Recommendation:** Do not build a large content estate before the site has proven a clear conversion path.
- **Recommendation:** Do not use the website to imply mature proof, mature integrations, or mature product breadth that the current truth base cannot support.
- **Recommendation:** Do not let audience breadth become an excuse for vague positioning.
- **Recommendation:** Do not let the site become a generic fleet-management brand exercise detached from FleetHive’s actual current product and commercial priority.

## Measurement implications

- **Recommendation:** Measurement must follow the conversion ladder, not just top-level visits.
- **Recommendation:** Because likely traffic sources include paid ads, outbound sales, SEO, and PR, measurement should be source-aware and segment-aware wherever possible.

### Minimum funnel instrumentation to plan for

- **Recommendation:** Landing page viewed
- **Recommendation:** Segment selected or audience path chosen, if routing is introduced
- **Recommendation:** Primary CTA clicked
- **Recommendation:** Sign-up / onboarding started
- **Recommendation:** Sign-up / account creation completed
- **Recommendation:** First meaningful activation step completed, such as adding a vehicle
- **Recommendation:** Subscription started or plan selected, where applicable
- **Recommendation:** Assisted-route request submitted, if demo or walkthrough remains live

### Page-level diagnostics to monitor

- **Recommendation:** Home-page CTA click-through by traffic source
- **Recommendation:** Drop-off between homepage and onboarding entry
- **Recommendation:** Drop-off inside the onboarding entry flow
- **Recommendation:** Performance of household versus small-business routes if both remain in scope
- **Recommendation:** Difference between raw traffic quality and activation quality by source

### Metric interpretation rules

- **Recommendation:** A higher signup-start rate is not enough if completion and activation quality worsen.
- **Recommendation:** A lower top-of-funnel volume may still represent success if the site improves fit, reduces confusion, and sends more qualified users into the product.
- **Placeholder:** The “500 signups” number can later be turned into a measurable target only after baseline traffic quality, conversion rate, and activation data are known.
- **Unknown / needs validation:** Current baseline funnel numbers are not yet present in the canonical file set.
